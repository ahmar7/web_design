Visualizing Health Equity in Southeast Asia

These visualizations aim to simplify the concept, highlighting potential leverages at policy or programmatic levels in Southeast Asia.

Health equity means different things to different people and communities, especially because it all depends on key social, economic, cultural and political factors to define social equity. Whether it's access to affordable food, adequate transportation systems, cohesion and tolerance, overall financial stability, type of climate, or something completely unrelated to any of these suggestions, the definition of health and social equity is subjective.

Through this project, we intend to operationalise the concept of food security in Southeast Asia by identifying key indicators which could be monitored and visualized for strategy and actions. Conflict and food insecurity

The study of association between conflict and food insecurity has gained prominence in recent years. This trend was seen to increase directly for Indonesia and Cambodia where conflicts increased with food security, whereas the trend was inverse for Philippines. However it is also noted that this could be related to other political aspects within the country and food security is likely to be a confounding factor.
